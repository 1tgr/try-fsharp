Try F#

Thanks, Hacker News!

By Tim Robinson on January 18, 2011 10:53 PM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

I got some great beta testingfeedback on the Hacker News comments for this site. Try F# still relatively stable, although I'd like to go back and make the fsi.exe launching process a little more robust; I'm having to shut down old processes by hand.

Unfortunately my amateur web programming skills have prevented the site from working in IE8 at the moment, likely due to HttpOnly cookies not being passed back to Javascript. The only excuse I can attempt is that I've been testing on two browsers more commonly available on Mac OS X.

I'm planning on re-doing the Ajax mechanism -- which currently polls once a second for console output from the fsi.exe process -- and replacing it with long polling against a database of text output. That way I can decouple fsi.exe from the web app, and I can remove dependence on ASP.NET's session cookie.

Thanks all for your constructive feedback, and thanks in particular to IE users for your patience...
